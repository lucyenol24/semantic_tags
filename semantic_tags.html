Mountains Near Seattle, WA

Top 3 Mountains To Climb

Mount Rainier is the most famous of the Washington Mountains. Standing at 14,410 feet, only those who are skilled in mountaineering can even attempt to summit this beauty.

Mount Baker is nestled deep in the north cascades, near Bellingham, Washington. It is a popular destination for climbers, as well as skiers.

Mount Shuksan is my personal favorite of the three listed mountains. It is beautiful, but dangerous. It is easily viewed from Mt. Baker ski area, as well as Artist Point nearby.

Lesser Known Washington Mountains

Climbing mountains in Washington is an incredibly rewarding, but dangerous sport. It is important to know how to climb before attempting to summit any of these mountains. A great place to learn how to not only alpine climb, but to learn rock climbing, navigation, and alpine first aid, is the Mountaineers Club in Seattle, Washington.

The Mountaineers are a volunteer-based group of outdoorsy folks who dedicate their time to helping others enjoy the outdoors in a fun and safe manner. If you become a member, a year long alpine climbing class is only $500, taught by some of the most experienced climbers around. Fred Beckey, a famous rock climber, is a member of the club and often shows up at the free climbs.
